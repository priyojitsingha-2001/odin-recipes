Mutton Kosha recipe

To make authentic Bengali Mutton Kosha, start by marinating mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt for at least 2 hours. Heat mustard oil in a pan until it smokes, then temper with whole garam masala (bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom). Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Stir in ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and ground spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala. Cook until the oil separates. Add the marinated mutton and sauté on medium heat until it is well-coated and releases its juices. Cover and slow-cook until tender, occasionally adding water for a rich, thick gravy. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with steamed rice or parathas.

Ingredients

  • Mutton: 500 grams
  • Yogurt: 2 tbps
  • Mustard oil: 4 tbsp
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium (chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 2 tbsp
  • Turmeric powder: 1 tsp
  • Red chili powder: 1 tsp
  • cumin powder: 1tsp
  • coriander powder: 1tsp
  • Garam masala: 1tsp
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom): for tempering
  • Salt: to taste
  • Fresh coriander: for garnish

Here are the steps to make Mutton Kosha:

  1. Marinate the Mutton: Mix mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 2 hours (overnight for better flavor).
  2. Heat Mustard Oil: In a heavy-bottomed pan or kadhai, heat mustard oil until it smokes lightly. This removes its raw aroma.
  3. Temper the Oil: Add whole spices (bay leaves, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom) to the hot oil and sauté for a few seconds until aromatic.
  4. Fry Onions: Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Cook the Masala: Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears. Add chopped tomatoes, cumin powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook the mixture until the oil separates.
  6. Sauté the Mutton: Add the marinated mutton to the pan and sauté on medium heat. Stir frequently until the mutton is well-coated in the masala and starts releasing its juices.
  7. Slow Cook: Lower the heat, cover the pan, and let the mutton cook slowly. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Add water in small amounts if needed, for a thick, rich gravy.
  8. Final Touch: Once the mutton is tender and the gravy is thick, garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Serve Hot: Enjoy Mutton Kosha with steamed rice, basmati pulao, or flaky parathas!
